Project details

The aim of this project is to investigate the prediction accuracy of prediction markets in the context of scientific research. Within the next few months 18 prominently published studies in experimental economics will be replicated by research teams from Caltech, the University of Innsbruck and the National University of Singapore [comment a]. These studies have been objectively chose because the are laboratory studies with student participants published in the American Economic Review and in the Quarterly Journal of Economics in the years 2011 to 2013, testing specific hypotheses in between-subjects designs. From each study one between-subjects hypothesis is taken according to the following criteria:

  1. Include the most “important” statistically significant between-subjects results/hypothesis in the experiment based on the results highlighted by the authors and our own reading.
  2. If several statistically significant between-subjects results/hypotheses of about equal importance in the paper; include the result/hypothesis concerned with efficiency.
  3. If several statistically significant between subjects results/hypotheses still remain we randomly picked on. In the prediction markets the traders can give their assessment of whether a result will be replicated or not. For each study, a separate prediction market is run to predict the outcome of replication. In these markets assets are traded whose payoffs depend on whether the results will be replicated or not. For more information on the replicated studies and the hypotheses click here.

Trading and all survey questions are anonymous - the company hosting the prediction markets will provide us with de-personalized data.

The markets will be open for trading on Mar 17 20:00 Central European Time and close on Mar 31 20:00 Central European Time.
